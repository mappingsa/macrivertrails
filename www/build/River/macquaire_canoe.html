Documented by the NSW Department of Lands in 2009 and described as follows on their web site. Starting at Wellington, the six day Macquarie River Canoe Trail travels 150 kms to Narromine and winds its way through areas lined with huge old river red gums, providing habitat for a vast range of birds and animals with some being quite rare or unique to the area. Good water flows from the Burrendong Dam provide excellent conditions during the summer months, and riverside reserves provide easy river access and relaxing camping spots.How to go about this activity: This is a self-guided trail. You can download the detailed planning guide, see web site for link. Ideal starting locations are the Riverside Caravan Park or the Bridge Motel in Wellington (see River bedz). You will need to bring your own canoe, etc. Hiring is not an option at present.
The local WomDomNom Canoe and Kayak Club runs an annual Macquarie river paddle from Wellington to Dubbo which is on the first weekend in December. Go to www.womdomnom.com for details
